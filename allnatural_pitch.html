Your curl, specifically
From 3B waves to 4C coils — all covered.
AllNatural adapts its advice, moisture strategy, and product matching to your exact curl pattern. Not a generic "curly hair" category.
3B
3B — Defined spiral curls
Springy, voluminous curls with a circumference close to a Sharpie marker. The cuticle is more open than 4-type hair, meaning products penetrate easily but moisture also escapes faster in humidity or dry climates.
Key needs: frizz control, humidity protection, light sealing oils. Avoid heavy butters — they flatten the curl pattern.
3C
3C — Tight corkscrew curls
Dense, corkscrew coils close together. Significant shrinkage (up to 50%). Prone to dryness because the tighter spiral slows the travel of natural scalp oils down the shaft.
Key needs: regular deep conditioning, leave-in moisture, protective styling between washes. LOC method works well here.
4A
4A — Defined S-pattern coils
Tight S-shaped coils with a visible curl pattern even when dry. High shrinkage (50–60%). Sebum travels very little distance from the scalp — moisture must be applied intentionally.
Key needs: consistent moisture routine, satin protection at night, protective styles for length retention. Porosity assessment is critical here.
4B
4B — Z-pattern coils
Hair bends in sharp angles rather than curls. Extremely high shrinkage (60–75%). Low definition when dry. Fragile at the bends — manipulation without moisture causes breakage at the Z-point specifically.
Key needs: stretch styling to reduce manipulation, heavy sealing, protective styles. Never detangle dry. Deep condition weekly.
4C
4C — Tightly coiled, densest texture
The tightest coil pattern with the least defined curl when dry. Shrinkage 70–80%+. The cuticle angle is nearly perpendicular to the shaft — scalp sebum travels almost zero distance. Every inch of length requires intentional effort to retain.
Key needs: LOC method on saturated-wet hair, regular scalp massages, ferritin and iron monitoring, biweekly wash maximum. High-manipulation styles accelerate breakage.

The science behind it
Not advice. Evidence.
🧪
Khumalo et al. (2007): 17.1% prevalence of traction alopecia in a Black African population — directly informing AllNatural's tension tracking and scalp rest recommendations after style take-downs.
🧪
Panahi et al. (2015): Rosemary oil applied topically showed equivalent efficacy to minoxidil 2% for hair growth in a randomised controlled trial — the basis for every castor + rosemary scalp massage recommendation in the app.
🧪
Harrison & Bergfeld (2009): Cortisol elevation pushes hair follicles from anagen (growth) to telogen (shedding) within 8–12 weeks — why AllNatural tracks stress monthly and plots it against your shedding ratings.
🧪
Franbourg et al. (2003) & Robbins (2013): African hair has the highest cuticle lift angle of any hair type — up to 90° from the shaft — explaining why sebum barely travels and why moisture-sealing is structurally necessary, not optional.
🧪
Trost et al. (2006) & WHO data: Serum ferritin below 40ng/mL causes non-scarring alopecia even without clinical anaemia. Iron deficiency is common across African diaspora populations globally — especially postpartum. AllNatural prompts users to test ferritin specifically, not just general iron.
